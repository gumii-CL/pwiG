Who we are

Our Mission

Frida is an iGEM 2026 project from Seville, Spain. We are a multidisciplinary team of students working at the intersection of synthetic biology, bioinformatics, and healthcare equity.

Hundreds of millions of people worldwide live with a rare disease, yet most screening programs cover only a handful of conditions. Early diagnosis can be life-changing — but only if every newborn has access to it, regardless of postcode or economic status.

We believe diagnostic inequality is not inevitable. Frida combines CRISPR-Cas13 collateral cleavage with combinatorial guide RNA logic to build a scalable, multiplexed biosensor that can screen for many rare diseases from a single dried blood spot.

CRISPR-SHERLOCK

Harnessing Cas13's collateral cleavage for ultra-sensitive fluorescent detection of pathogenic variants.

Multiplexed design

Combinatorial gRNA logic in a multi-well format — identify dozens of variants from a single sample.

gRNA Design App

An open-source web tool that takes HGVS / rsID / OMIM input and ranks optimized CRISPR spacer sequences.

Equitable access

Designed with low-resource settings in mind — minimal equipment, low cost per sample, scalable protocol.

How it works

The Platform at a Glance

From a drop of neonatal blood to a fluorescent readout — Frida's workflow is designed to be fast, reliable, and easy to interpret.

01

Sample prep

Dried blood spot collected at birth. DNA extracted and amplified via RPA (isothermal).

02

gRNA selection

Disease-specific spacers designed with our open-source app, targeting pathogenic variants.

03

CRISPR detection

Cas13 recognizes the target RNA, activates, and cleaves fluorescent reporter probes.

04

Readout

Fluorescence plate reader interprets combinatorial pattern — result in under two hours.
